Entry Barrier
$499/month locked out the entire freelancer market.
For a solo consultant on a ₹20K/month tool budget, Scout was a non-starter. The brain never got to evaluate the product. The seat model charged for a chair before the user had seen the room.
Value Leakage
Stimulus did the work of 3 researchers. It charged for 1 seat.
When a diagnostic tool automates what three researchers did manually, charging a flat seat fee lets the vendor capture only a fraction of the value delivered. Usage-based pricing captures the upside that flat seats structurally give away.
Compute-Blind Billing
A gaze check and a full fMRI scan both cost "1 pair."
The compute gap between them is 60×. TRIBE v2 (28–32 GB VRAM) vs a 2D CNN saliency pass are not the same operation. Charging the same price for both creates structural margin loss at every fMRI-heavy account.
Dead Upgrade Zone
$499 → $1,900 is a 3.8× cliff with no bridge.
A growing agency that outgrew Scout had one option: a $1,400/month step-change. No intermediate tier, no booster packs. Agencies churned rather than upgraded — killing the expansion revenue path entirely.

§02 · New Pricing Architecture07 / 19
The Standard Value Metric
$1.00 USD = 1,000 SNT
SNT (Stimulus Neuro-Tokens) is the platform's native billing currency. One transparent conversion rate. No per-seat fees. No resolution tiers. You pay for the cognitive modalities you activate and the duration of the clip you scan — nothing else.
Eye Gaze · Visual Salience
10 SNT/sec
Low compute. 2D CNN saliency on normalised 224×224 frames. Predicts where the eye lands in the first 250ms. Ideal for visual hierarchy and thumb-stop checks.
Facial Expression · Emotion AI
15 SNT/sec
Moderate GPU. Frame-by-frame facial Action Unit parsing. Tracks micro-expression engagement and emotional arousal valleys across the clip timeline.
Voice Sentiment · Acoustics
15 SNT/sec
Moderate GPU. Dual-recurrent encoder processes audio and speech simultaneously. Extracts acoustic pitch, resonance, and confidence vectors in real time.
Simulated fMRI Projection
60 SNT/sec
High compute. Loads Meta's TRIBE v2 trimodal model across 28–32 GB VRAM. Maps emotional reward (amygdala) and processing friction (prefrontal cortex) — the deepest diagnostic available.
Note on pricing: SNT rates above are indicative and based on current GPU benchmark estimates. Final per-modality rates will be recalibrated once COGS (compute cost per inference) is fully instrumented. This model serves as a structural framework for pricing design — actual margins will be validated against real usage data before public launch.

Layer 3 · Value Proposition & Positioning
The job isn't to test content.
It's to stop burning the media budget. Three jobs, one platform.
Functional Job
"Stop the thumb."
"I need to know if our 15-second ad hook will stop a user's thumb in under 0.3 seconds — before I waste our client's media budget." The scroll-stop probability in the first 0.3s is worth more than any post-launch A/B test.
Emotional Job
"Pitch with proof, not opinion."
"I want to walk into a client meeting with a brain scan that says why Creative B wins — not a creative director's gut. I want scientific confidence, not a debate." Stimulus turns subjectivity into a defensible number.
Operational Job
"Cut 12 hours to 2."
"I need to reduce our content testing and revision loop from 12–18 hours down to under 2 hours." Entropik reports 60% reduction in time-to-insight. Stimulus targets the same benchmark, accessible at ₹12 per scan.
Pillar 3 · Institutional Rigor & Agency Partnerships
⬡  Verified by Stimulus
Agencies running high-spend campaigns for Tata, Nikon, Reliance receive co-branded neural validation reports stamped with this badge. The badge means: this creative was validated at the cortical level before any media budget was committed. Biometric proof — not a creative director's gut.
Retainer → Performance Contract
When an agency can show pre-spend cortical validation, they move from flat retainers to performance-based fees — capturing a % of improved client ROI. Stimulus becomes the instrument that justifies the performance premium.
The Moat
Once an agency's clients expect "Verified by Stimulus" reports, switching away means losing the badge — and the scientific authority it carries in board-level reviews. Rigor creates switching cost without lock-in tactics.
